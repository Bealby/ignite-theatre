Having been out of the theatrical loop for many years I was keen to take the reins in finding a play that I loved with those who I loved working with.

In February 2020 some friends met for a read through of Memory of Water by Shelagh Stephenson and steps were taken to perform in May that year without knowing that the omnipotent Covid was waiting in the wings!

Tenancity, passion and a little bit of madness kept us ploughing through the obstacles that presented in the year, that followed as we dealt with casting chaos as folk had to drop out for various reasons, venue and date changes and of course the peaks and the plains of rehearsing on zoom and then Lewes Priory ruins in all weathers.

All was as if all the set backs made us want to pursue our goal with even more fervour and so we are very proud of Ignite Theatre, born out of lockdown and look forward to presenting our first production when the country is on the cusp of 'opening up' again.
