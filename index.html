What is the play all about?

Sit back, relax and enjoy peeking into the ways that three charming, acid-tongued and zany sisters manage relationships in a time of crisis and uncertainty. Secrets and lies unfold as they meet up at their late mother’s home by the sea for her funeral. The Memory of Water is a beautifully observed, award-winning comical play by Shelagh Stephenson (Laurence Olivier Award for Best New Comedy 2000) with writing that can take you from sublime family reunion to comic chaos in one swoop.

Who are we?

Some may think lockdown as a curious, perhaps mildly mad, time to organise a production and it hasn’t been an easy journey. With three venue and date changes, we also faced three different Marys in the cast, and the joys and tribulations of rehearsing on zoom, in yurts, gardens and parks, all with social distancing.

With the omnipotent covid lurking in the wings we navigated through the stormy weathers with tenacity and passion as our weapons. In many ways the set backs made us pursue our goal with even more fervour and so we are very proud of Ignite Theatre, born out of lockdown in 2020 by a local troupe team who look forward to presenting our first production in July 2021 as the country opens up again.
